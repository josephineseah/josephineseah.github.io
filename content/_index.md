# Hi, I'm Jo. 

I recently completed my PhD in Sociology at the University of Cambridge. 

For my doctoral research, I studied how people *actually* live with smart city technologies. By this I mean: smart city projects are usually designed around ideas of optimization and efficiency, thus creating expectations around how users should behave. In practice, users often use technologies in a variety of ways, often in ways outside their intended design. Over the course of my PhD, I drew on qualitative methods to study the gap between the visions of smart cities--seamless and friction-free--and their actual implementation, focusing on the lived experiences of city residents and the implications of this gap on digital inclusion and urban liveability.
